The Consequences

Courts are already sanctioning AI hallucinations

When AI-generated falsehoods reach courtrooms, regulatory filings, or professional reports, the consequences are no longer hypothetical. Attorneys have been sanctioned, cases dismissed, and regulators have taken enforcement action.

ByoPlanet v. Johansson
$86,626 sanctions
Largest AI hallucination sanction to date. Attorney relied on ChatGPT-generated brief containing fabricated case citations. Court imposed the full cost of opposing counsel's fees.
E.D. Va. · Aug 2025
Fletcher v. Experian
$2,500 fine · 21 fabrications
5th Circuit found 21 fabricated case citations in a single filing. Court imposed sanctions and referred attorney for disciplinary investigation.
5th Cir. · Feb 2026
Noland v. Land of the Free
$10,000 sanction · 21 of 23 citations fabricated
California Appeals court found 21 of 23 cited cases were entirely fictitious. Attorney had submitted the AI output without any verification.
Cal. App. · 2025
MyPillow / Lindell Case
$3,000 per attorney · 20+ errors
Multiple attorneys sanctioned for AI-generated filings containing fabricated citations and non-existent legal precedents. Court noted pattern of unverified AI reliance.
D. Minn. · Jul 2025
FTC vs. DoNotPay & Rytr
Regulatory enforcement
FTC cracked down on AI tools generating fabricated legal documents and deceptive content. Enforcement actions established that AI hallucinations in consumer-facing products trigger regulatory liability.
FTC · 2024–2025
Boston v. Williams
17 of 24 citations hallucinated
Northern District of Georgia found the vast majority of cited legal authorities were fabricated by AI. Highlighted the systematic nature of LLM citation hallucination.
N.D. Ga. · 2024
